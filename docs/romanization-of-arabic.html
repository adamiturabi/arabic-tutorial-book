Romanization of Arabic

The following romanization scheme is used in this book for the transcription of Arabic sounds.

consonants

ء ب ت ث ج ح خ د ذ ر ز س ش ص
ʾ b t t͡h j ḥ k͡h d d͡h r z s s͡h ṣ
ض ط ظ ع غ ف ق ک ل م ن ه و ي
ḍ ṭ ḍ͡h ɛ g͡h f q k l m n h w y

vowels

◌َا ◌ُو ◌ِي ◌َوْ ◌َيْ
ā ū ī aw ay

When transcribing example text, the italic Latin script is used, no letters are capitalized, a non-sentence-initial connecting hamzah (همزة الوصل) is written with ٱ in Arabic and transcribed with a hyphen (-), and the lām of the definite article is not transliterated. Tāʾ marbūṭah ة is transcribed as t except at the end of an utterance, when it is transcribed as h. For example:

بِسمِ ٱللَّـٰهِ ٱلرَّحْمَـٰنِ ٱلرَّحِيمِ.
bismi -llāhi -rraḥmāni -rraḥīm.

When using Arabic words in the English text, the regular (non-italic) Latin script is used, words are transcribed in pausa pronunciation, the first letter of proper nouns are capitalized, word-initial hamzah is not transcribed, the definite article is always transcribed as “al”, and a hyphen is inserted following the definite article. Tāʾ marbūṭah ة is transcribed as “h” except when in construct, where is transcribed as “t”. Accurate transcriptions are preferred unless an established and distinctly separate usage is current in English. For example:

“Allāh revealed Sūrat al-Fatiḥah in the Qurʾān.”

“The caliphate moved from al-Madīnah to Damascus.”
