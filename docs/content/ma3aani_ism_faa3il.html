29  The meanings of the doer participle

29.1 Introduction

The verb essentially signifies the occurrence of an action. Consider the following example:

  1. يَتَّبِعُ زَيْدٌ عَمْرًا

The u-state stateful verb يَتَّبِعُ signifies an imperfect (i.e. incomplete) action that can have one of the following tenses:

  1. Zayd is following Ɛamr. (Present continuous tense)
  2. Zayd was following Ɛamr. (Past continuous tense. Typically as كَانَ يَتَّبِعُ but remember from section (ref) that كَانَ can be omitted with the stateful verb if the context indicates a past tense.)
  3. Zayd follows Ɛamr. (Present habitual tense)
  4. Zayd used to follow Ɛamr. (Past habitual tense. Again, typically as كَانَ يَتَّبِعُ.)
  5. Zayd will follow Ɛamr. (Future tense)

Similarly if we say:

  1. اتَّبَعَ زَيْدٌ عَمْرًا
    Zayd followed Ɛamr.

then the perfect verb اتَّبَعَ signifies the past tense.

In contrast to the verb, the doer participle مُتَّبِع, essentially signifies that its doer can be called a “follower”. For example, if we say:

  1. زَيْدٌ مُتَّبِعُ عَمْرٍو

Then the doer participle مُتَّبِع signifies that Zayd is, was, or will be, Ɛamr’s follower.

In example (3) the doer participle مُتَّبِع can be used with verbal force. What we mean by verbal force is that the doer participle, while being a noun, can also signify some aspects of meaning that are typically supplied by the verb. These aspects are:

  1. the occurence of the action of verb,
  2. and its tense.

So the doer participle مُتَّبِع, while essentially meaning “follower”, can also supply the meaning of the occurrence of an action of “following”. As for the tense of when the following occurred, the doer participle does not carry tense per se. But the tense of its verbal meaning can be determined from context, the syntax of doer participle, and the lexical meaning of its root verb.

Depending on the tense, the annexation مُتَّبِعُ عَمْرٍو in example (3) above may be either a real or superficial annexation. (See section (ref).) If the context indicates a past tense, then it is a real annexation.

But if the context indicates that the tense is future, continuous, or habitual, then the annexation can be superficial, and عَمْرٍ is then in the position of a direct doee. In this case, the sentence can also be expressed as:

  1. زَيْدٌ مُتَّبِعٌ عَمْرًا

Here is an example from actual usage:

  1. وَأَنَا مُتَّبِعٌ فِي ذَلِكَ قَضَاءَ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ صلى الله عليه وسلم فِي مَرْيَمَ الْمَغَالِيَّةِ [سنن النسائي :3498]

In example (5) above, context tells us that the tense of the verbal meaning in the doer participle مُتَّبِع is the present tense. When translating this sentence, we have something of a dilemma. If we translate the doer participle مُتَّبِع as “follower”, then we will say:

  1. I am a follower, in that [matter], of the ruling of the Messenger of Allāh ﷺ concerning Maryam al-Mag͡hāliyyah.

But now we will lose the tense of the verbal meaning included in مُتَّبِع. If, on the other hand, we translate مُتَّبِع with the verb “following”, then we get:

  1. I am following, in that [matter], the ruling of the Messenger of Allāh ﷺ concerning Maryam al-Mag͡hāliyyah

Now we can lose the essential significance of the doer participle which is that of the noun “follower”.

Many times, the verbal meaning is prioritized for the translation and so the doer participle, when used with verbal force, is often translated as a verb. But we should understand that this is a limitation of translation to English, and that the original Arabic, in general, has aspects of meaning of both a noun and a verb.

Also, the verbal force that an doer participle carries can vary on a sliding scale. Sometimes, the doer participle’s essential doer meaning is more dominant. For example:

  1. لَعَنَ اللَّهُ الْخَمْرَ وَشَارِبَهَا وَسَاقِيَهَا وَبَائِعَهَا وَمُبْتَاعَهَا وَعَاصِرَهَا وَمُعْتَصِرَهَا وَحَامِلَهَا وَالْمَحْمُولَةَ إِلَيْهِ [سنن أبي داود :3674]
    Allah has cursed wine, its drinker, its server, its seller, its buyer, its presser, the one for whom it is pressed, the one who conveys it, and the one to whom it is conveyed.

And sometimes its verbal (tense) meaning is more dominant. For example:

  1. جَاءَتْ مَلاَئِكَةٌ إِلَى النَّبِيِّ صلى الله عليه وسلم وَهْوَ نَائِمٌ فَقَالَ بَعْضُهُمْ إِنَّهُ نَائِمٌ وَقَالَ بَعْضُهُمْ إِنَّ الْعَيْنَ نَائِمَةٌ وَالْقَلْبَ يَقْظَانُ [صحيح البخاري :7281]
    [Some] angels came to the Prophet ﷺ while he was sleeping. Some of them said, “He is sleeping.” Some [others] of them said, “The eye is sleeping but the heart is awake.”

In the next few sections we will look at more examples of doer participles in real-world usage and examine the verbal meanings that the doer participle signifies.

29.2 The doer participle without ٱلْ used to indicate a imperfect static state

One of the more common usages of the doer participle is to indicate that the doer is in an imperfect static state. The term static state here means remaining in a stationary condition or position as opposed to a dynamic action.

The doer participle indicates being in a state from a semantic aspect. And we have already studied that the ḥāl syntactically indicates being in a state. These two (semantic and syntactic) aspects of indicating being in a state are often combined. So, we will see from the examples in this section that the doer participle is often (but not always) either a ḥāl, or part of a ḥāl sentence.

As we discuss the state indicated by an doer participle we will often deal with stative verbs. Stative verbs are those which mean to be in a state. For example: جَلَسَ to sit, قَامَ to stand, نَامَ to sleep, لَبِسَ to wear, etc. These verbs often mean both: to do an action of entering into a state, and also to remain in such a state. For example, جَلَسَ can mean both: to enter into a seated state (from another position like standing, etc.), and also to remain in a seated state. Similarly, لَبِسَ can mean both: to put on a garment, and also to be in a state of having put on the garment.

In contrast to the doer participle, the stateful verb indicates that the doer is doing a dynamic action. Consider the following example:

  1. كُنْتُ مُسَافِرًا فَأَتَيْتُ النَّبِيَّ صلى الله عليه وسلم وَهُوَ يَأْكُلُ وَأَنَا صَائِمٌ [سنن النسائي :2281]
    “I was traveling and I came to the Prophet while he was eating and I was fasting.”

The verb يَأْكُلُ indicates a dynamic activity, where the doer is actively eating. Whereas the doer participle صَائِم calls attention to the doer’s being in a (static) state of fasting, and not to his actively doing the activity of fasting.

Even when a verb that is associated with a dynamic activity, its doer participle can be used instead of the verb when the purpose is to indicate the doer’s being in a state of doing the activity, rather than to indicate the dynamic activity of the verb. For example:

  1. فَلَبِثَ الْأَوْسُ وَالْخَزْرَجُ مُتَحَارِبِينَ عِشْرِينَ سَنَةً فِي أَمْرِ سُمَيْرٍ [marmor 134]
    “So al-ʾAws and al-K͡hazraj stayed (in a state of) fighting each other for twenty years because of the matter of Sumayr.”

  2. فَمَا الَّذِي أَنْتَ فِيهِ الْآنَ مُجْمِعٌ [marmor 99A]
    “So what is it that you are now (in a state of being) decided about?

Here are some more examples of the the doer participle being used to indicate being in a static state:

  1. لَمَّا كَانَ يَوْمَ أُحُدٍ انْهَزَمَ النَّاسُ عَنِ النَّبِيِّ صلى الله عليه وسلم وَأَبُو طَلْحَةَ بَيْنَ يَدَىِ النَّبِيِّ صلى الله عليه وسلم مُجَوِّبٌ عَلَيْهِ بِحَجَفَةٍ لَهُ [صحيح البخاري :4064]
    “When it was the day of Uḥud, the people left the Prophet ﷺ while Abū Ṭalḥah was in front of the Prophet ﷺ, shielding him with a leather shield of his.”

  2. فَخَرَجَ النَّبِيُّ صلى الله عليه وسلم وَهُوَ مُشْتَمِلٌ عَلَى شَيْءٍ [جامع الترمذي :3769]
    “So the Prophet ﷺ came out while he was (in a state of) covering something [with his garment].”

  3. وَإِذَا هُوَ مُعْتَمِدٌ عَلَى عَصًا فِي صَلاَتِهِ فَقُلْنَا بَعْدَ أَنْ سَلَّمْنَا . فَقَالَ حَدَّثَتْنِي أُمُّ قَيْسٍ بِنْتُ مِحْصَنٍ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صلى الله عليه وسلم لَمَّا أَسَنَّ وَحَمَلَ اللَّحْمَ اتَّخَذَ عَمُودًا فِي مُصَلاَّهُ يَعْتَمِدُ عَلَيْهِ [سنن أبي داود :948]
    “And incidentally, he was resting on a staff in his prayer. We [asked] him (about resting on the staff) after we had given the salutation; He said: Umm Qays daughter of Miḥṣan said to me that when the Messenger of Allah ﷺ became aged and the flesh grew increasingly on him, he took a prop at his place of prayer and he would rest on it.”
    (Note how the doer participle مُعْتَمِد indicates a state and the stateful verb يَعْتَمِدُ indicates the activity.”

  4. فَبَيْنَا أَنَا نَازِلٌ تَحْتَ شَجَرَةٍ إِذَا رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صلى الله عليه وسلم [موطأ مالك :48]
    While I was in a dismounted state under a tree, the Messenger of Allah [came]

  5. إِذَا مَرِضَ الْعَبْدُ أَوْ سَافَرَ، كُتِبَ لَهُ مِثْلُ مَا كَانَ يَعْمَلُ مُقِيمًا صَحِيحًا [صحيح البخاري :56]
    When a slave journeys or falls ill, a [similar reward] is written for him as what he used to do while staying (i.e. not journeying) [and] healthy.

  6. إِنَّ أُمِّي قَدِمَتْ وَهْىَ رَاغِبَةٌ [صحيح البخاري :5979]
    My mother has arrived and she is desirous (of my favor).

29.2.1 The direct doee of the doer participle when indicating a static state

When the doer participle is dervied from a transitive verb, then the doer participle, when indicating a static state, may take a direct doee (as long as it satisifes the conditions specified in section (ref)). For example:

  1. مَنْ كَانَ مِنْكُمْ مُلْتَمِسًا لَيْلَةَ الْقَدْرِ فَلْيَلْتَمِسْهَا فِي الْعَشْرِ الْأَوَاخِرِ وِتْرًا [مسند أحمد :298]
    “Whoever amongst you is seeking the Night of Decree, then let him seek it in the last ten days of Ramaḍān, [in the] the odd [nights].”
    (Note the the doer participle مُلْتَمِسًا has a direct doee لَيْلَةَ الْقَدْرِ.)

  2. إِنَّهُ كَانَ يُصَلِّي وَهُوَ مُسْبِلٌ إِزَارَهُ [سنن أبي داود :638, graded weak by al-ʾAlbāni]
    “Indeed he was praying while trailing his lower garment.”

  3. وَوَقَفْتُ عِنْدَ بَابِ عَائِشَةَ فَإِذَا هُوَ مُسْتَقْبِلٌ الْمَغْرِبَ [الأدب المفرد :451]
    “And I stopped at the door of Ɛāʾis͡hah and incidentally, it was facing the west.”

  4. كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صلى الله عليه وسلم حَامِلَ الْحَسَنِ بْنِ عَلِيٍّ عَلَى عَاتِقِهِ [جامع الترمذي :3784]
    “The Messenger of Allah ﷺ was carrying al-Ḥasan ibn Ɛaliyy on his shoulder”
    (حَامِلَ الْحَسَنِ is a superficial annexation.)

  5. أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صلى الله عليه وسلم كَانَ يُصَلِّي وَهُوَ حَامِلٌ أُمَامَةَ [سنن النسائي :1204]
    “that the Messenger of Allah used to pray while he [was] carrying Umāmah”

  6. أَنَّ أَبَا بَكْرٍ اسْتَأْذَنَ عَلَى رَسُولِ اللَّهِ صلى الله عليه وسلم وَهُوَ مُضْطَجِعٌ عَلَى فِرَاشِهِ لاَبِسٌ مِرْطَ عَائِشَةَ [صحيح مسلم :2402a]
    “that Abū Bakr sought permission from the Messenger of Allah ﷺ [for entrance in his apartment] while he was lying on his bed having on him Ɛāʾis͡hah’s bed-sheet.”

However, when the main intent is to indicate the state of the doer independent of the action of the doer upon any doee, then the doer participle can occur without a direct doee. For example:

  1. قَالَ عُثْمَانُ أَجَلْ وَلَكِنَّا كُنَّا خَائِفِينَ [مسند أحمد :431]
    Ɛut͡hman said, “Yes, but we were in a state of fear.”

In such cases, the doer participle can be followed with a verb (typically its own), specifying the direct doee. For example:

  1. فَخَرَجْنَا خَائِفِينَ نَخَافُ الرَّصَدَ [maghazi 28 cited by Marmorstein, M., Tense and text in Classical Arabic 138]
    “So we set out afraid, fearing ambush.”

  2. وَرُزَيْقٌ عَامِلٌ عَلَى أَرْضٍ يَعْمَلُهَا [صحيح البخاري :893]
    “Ruzayq was (in a state of) working on the land, working (i.e. farming) it.”

29.2.2 The doer participle indicating a habitual state

The static state that is indicated by the doer participle often tends to be continuous rather than habitual. But the doer participle can also indicate a habitual state. For example:

  1. عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ أَنَّهُ قَالَ لَا يَدْخُلُ الْجَنَّةَ مَنَّانٌ وَلَا عَاقٌّ وَالِدَيْهِ وَلَا مُدْمِنُ خَمْرٍ [https://hadithunlocked.com/ahmad:6882]
    [It was narrated] from the Prophet ﷺ that he said: “One who reminds people (of what he has given them) will not enter Paradise, nor will one who disobeys his parents, nor one addicted to wine.”

29.2.3 The doer participle for verbs of posture

Verbs of posture inlude: جَلَسَ to sit, قَامَ to stand, اضْطَجَعَ to lie down, etc.

With such verbs, the stateful verb, when used for the present or past, tends to be used only for the habitual tense and not for the continuous tense. So if we say يَجْلِسُ زَيْدٌ then it will usually mean Zayd sits and not Zayd is sitting.

For the continuous tense, only the doer participle tends to be used. So, in order to say Zayd is sitting we will usually say زَيْدٌ جَالِسٌ.

Here are some examples of the doer participle and stateful verb for verbs of posture:

The doer participle for verbs of posture indicating the continuous tense

  1. رَأَيْتُ النَّبِيَّ صلى الله عليه وسلم مُقْعِيًا يَأْكُلُ تَمْرًا [صحيح مسلم :2044a]
    “I saw the Prophet ﷺ squatting, eating dates.”

  2. قَالَ كُنْتُ مُتَّكِئًا عِنْدَ عَائِشَةَ [صحيح مسلم :177a]
    “I was resting at [the house of] Ɛāʾis͡hah”

  3. لاَ أُلْفِيَنَّ أَحَدَكُمْ مُتَّكِئًا عَلَى أَرِيكَتِهِ … [سنن أبي داود :4605]
    “Let me not find one of you reclining on his couch …”

  4. أَنَّ أَبَا بَكْرٍ رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ اسْتَأْذَنَ عَلَى رَسُولِ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ وَهُوَ مُضْطَجِعٌ عَلَى فِرَاشِهِ لَابِسٌ مِرْطَ عَائِشَةَ [مسند أحمد :514]
    “that Abū Bakr sought permission from the Messenger of Allah ﷺ [for entrance in his apartment] while he was lying on his bed having on him Ɛāʾis͡hah’s bed-sheet.”

  5. كُنْتُ جَالِسًا عِنْدَ مُعَاوِيَةَ، فَحَدَّثَ نَفْسَهُ ثُمَّ انْتَبَهَ [الأدب المفرد :564]
    “I was sitting with Muɛāwiyah when he [muttered to] himself, then he came to his senses.”

  6. إِنِّي لَجَالِسٌ مَعَ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ وَأَبِي مُوسَى وَهُمَا يَتَحَدَّثَانِ صحيح مسلم :2672d
    I was sitting with Ɛabd Allāh and Abū Mūsā while they were talking to each other.

  7. سَقَيْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صلى الله عليه وسلم مِنْ زَمْزَمَ فَشَرِبَ وَهُوَ قَائِمٌ [صحيح مسلم :2027a]
    I served the Messenger of Allah some Zamzam and he drank (it) while he was standing.

  8. هَلْ كَانَ النَّبِيُّ صلى الله عليه وسلم يُصَلِّي وَهُوَ قَاعِدٌ [صحيح مسلم :732a]
    Did the Prophet ﷺ pray while he was sitting?

The stateful verb for for verbs of posture indicating the habitual tense

  1. تِلْكَ صَلاَةُ الْمُنَافِقِ يَجْلِسُ يَرْقُبُ الشَّمْسَ حَتَّى إِذَا كَانَتْ بَيْنَ قَرْنَىِ الشَّيْطَانِ قَامَ فَنَقَرَ أَرْبَعًا لاَ يَذْكُرُ اللَّهَ فِيهَا إِلاَّ قَلِيلاً [جامع الترمذي :160]
    That is the prayer of the hypocrite. He sits watching the sun, until when it is between the horns of the devil he stands and pecks out four (units of prayer), not remembering Allah in them but a little.”

  2. كُنْتُ أَقْعُدُ مَعَ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ، يُجْلِسُنِي عَلَى سَرِيرِهِ [صحيح البخاري :53]
    I used to sit with Ibn Ɛabbās and he would seat me on his couch.

  3. كَانَ جِذْعٌ يَقُومُ إِلَيْهِ النَّبِيُّ صلى الله عليه وسلم [صحيح البخاري :918]
    [There] was a stem (of a date palm) [which] the Prophet ﷺ would stand against.

  4. وَيَرْكَعُ رَكْعَتَيْنِ قَبْلَ صَلاَةِ الْفَجْرِ ثُمَّ يَضْطَجِعُ عَلَى شِقِّهِ الأَيْمَنِ حَتَّى يَأْتِيَهُ الْمُؤَذِّنُ لِلصَّلاَةِ [صحيح البخاري :994]
    And he would pray two units [of (sunnah) prayer] before the Fajr prayer, then he would lie on his right side until the muʾad͡hd͡hin would come to him for the (obligatory) prayer”

Rarely, the stateful verb has been found to be used to indicate a continuous tense. But in such cases it will likely be in a ḥāl or with an adverb which will help indicate its continuous, rather than habitual, significance. Examples:

  1. أَنَّهُ رَأَى رَجُلاً يَتَّكِئُ عَلَى يَدِهِ الْيُسْرَى [سنن أبي داود :994]
    that he saw a man reclining on his left hand

  2. رَأَيْتُ عُمَرَ بْنَ الْخَطَّابِ وَهُوَ يَجْلِسُ وَالنَّاسُ مَعَهُ [tarikh tabari 3/429]
    I saw Ɛumar ibn al-K͡haṭṭāb while he [was] sitting while the people [were] with him

Transitive verbs of posture

The verbs of posture are usually intransitive verbs, as you can see in the examples so far. But a transitive verb of posture can also be used. For example:

  1. سَأَلْتُ ابْنَ عَبَّاسٍ وَهُوَ مُسْنِدٌ ظَهْرَهُ إِلَى الْكَعْبَةِ عَنِ الْبَاذَقِ [سنن النسائي :5687]
    I asked Ibn Ɛabbās, while he was leaning his back against the Kaɛbah, about (a drink called) al-Bād͡haq

  2. وَقَدْ كُنْتُ مُسْنِدَتَهُ إِلَى صَدْرِي [صحيح البخاري :2741]
    “I had been leaning him against my chest.”

  3. فَبَيْنَا أَنَا قَاعِدٌ وَرَسُولُ اللَّهِ صلى الله عليه وسلم مُتَوَسِّدٌ فَخِذِي [جامع الترمذي :2861]
    So while I was sitting and the Prophet ﷺ [was] using my thigh as a headrest

Other verbs that have similar behavior to the verbs of posture

In addition to verbs of posture, there are a few other verbs that indicate show the same distiction of the doer participle being used for the continous tense and the stateful verb for the habitual tense. For example: نَامَ to sleep, صَامَ to fast. The reason that these verbs show the same behavior as verbs of posture may be that the doer is not actively doing anything except remaining in a condition. Here are some examples with these verbs:

  1. قُلْنَا لِعَمْرٍو إِنَّ نَاسًا يَقُولُونَ إِنَّ النَّبِيَّ صلى الله عليه وسلم تَنَامُ عَيْنُهُ وَلاَ يَنَامُ قَلْبُهُ [صحيح البخاري :859]
    “We said to Ɛamr: ‘Indeed some people say that the Prophet ﷺ his eye sleeps but his heart does not sleep.’”

  2. جَاءَتْ مَلاَئِكَةٌ إِلَى النَّبِيِّ صلى الله عليه وسلم وَهْوَ نَائِمٌ فَقَالَ بَعْضُهُمْ إِنَّهُ نَائِمٌ وَقَالَ بَعْضُهُمْ إِنَّ الْعَيْنَ نَائِمَةٌ وَالْقَلْبَ يَقْظَانُ [صحيح البخاري :7281]
    Some angels came to the Prophet ﷺ while he was sleeping. Some of them said, “He is sleeping.” Some [others] of them said, “His eye[s] are sleeping but his heart is awake.”

  3. إِنِّي رَجُلٌ أَصُومُ أَفَأَصُومُ فِي السَّفَرِ [صحيح مسلم :1121d]
    “I am a man who [habitually] fasts, so should I fast in journeying?”

  4. سِرْنَا مَعَ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ صلى الله عليه وسلم وَهُوَ صَائِمٌ [صحيح مسلم :1101c]
    We travelled with the Messenger of Allah ﷺ while he [was] fasting.

29.2.4 The doer participle for verbs of motion

The doer participle is very common with verbs of motion, like: ذَهَبَ to go, مَشَى to walk, رَجَعَ to return, سَارَ to travel, etc. The doer participle for these verbs again indicates the situation of being in a move rather than the actual dynamic movement.1 For example:

  1. وَذَلِكَ الْمَسْجِدُ عَلَى حَافَةِ الطَّرِيقِ الْيُمْنَى، وَأَنْتَ ذَاهِبٌ إِلَى مَكَّةَ [صحيح البخاري :485]
    And that mosque is on the right edge of the path as you are going to Makkah

  2. كَانَ النَّبِيُّ صلى الله عليه وسلم يَأْتِي قُبَاءً رَاكِبًا وَمَاشِيًا [صحيح البخاري :1194]
    The Prophet ﷺ used to come to Qubāʾ while riding and while walking.

  3. لَحِقَنِي عَبَايَةُ بْنُ رَافِعٍ وَأَنَا مَاشٍ، إِلَى الْجُمُعَةِ [سنن النسائي :3116]
    Ɛabāyah ibn Rāfiɛ joined me while I was walking to the jumuɛah

  4. كُنْتُ مُسَافِرًا فَأَتَيْتُ النَّبِيَّ صلى الله عليه وسلم وَهُوَ يَأْكُلُ وَأَنَا صَائِمٌ [سنن النسائي :2281]
    “I was journeying and I came to the Prophet ﷺ while he was eating and I was fasting.”

  5. بَيْنَا النَّبِيُّ ﷺ سَائِرٌ إِلَى تَبُوكَ نَزَلَ عَنْ رَاحِلَتِهِ [https://hadithunlocked.com/bayhaqi:16844]
    “While the Prophet ﷺ was travelling to Tabūk, he dismounted off his mount”

  1. ثُمَّ أَرْدَفَنِي وَرَاءَهُ عَلَى الْعَضْبَاءِ رَاجِعِينَ إِلَى الْمَدِينَةِ [https://hadithunlocked.com/ahmad:16539]
    Then he seated me behind him on (his camel) al-Ɛaḍbāʾ while we [were] returning to al-Madīnah

  2. اتَّبَعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صلى الله عليه وسلم وَهُوَ رَاكِبٌ [سنن النسائي :953] [move to verbs of motion]
    “I followed the Messenger of Allah ﷺ when he was riding”

Unlike verbs of posture and temporary state, verbs of motion are frequently used for the continuous tense. Here are some examples:

  1. أَدْرَكَنِي أَبُو عَبْسٍ وَأَنَا أَذْهَبُ إِلَى الْجُمُعَةِ [صحيح البخاري :907]
    Abū Ɛabs overtook me while I [was] going to the jumuɛah

  2. بَيْنَا أَنَا أَسِيرُ مَعَ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ صلى الله عليه وسلم بَيْنَ الْجُحْفَةِ وَالأَبْوَاءِ إِذْ غَشِيَتْنَا رِيحٌ وَظُلْمَةٌ شَدِيدَةٌ سنن أبي داود :1463 While I was travelling with the Messenger of Allah ﷺ between al-Juḥfah and al-ʾAbwāʾ, suddenly a wind and intense darkness enveloped us

29.3 The doer participle without ٱلْ used to indicate a future event

In addition to indicating a static state, another verbal meaning of the doer participle is to indicate the future tense.

Here too, the doer participle is used instead of the stateful verb. When a stateful verb is used to indicate a future event, then it merely signifies that the speaker thinks that the doer will take an action. For example, the stateful verb أَذْهَبُ (when used to indicate the future tense) merely signifies the speaker’s intention of going some time in the future.

In contrast, when the doer participle is used to indicate the future tense, then it signifies that, in the estimation of the speaker, the event will definitely take place. In other words, the doer’s future action is so settled and resolved in the speaker’s mind that doer can already be described by the doer participle. So, for example, if the speaker says أَنَا ذَاهِبٌ and the context tells us he is referring to a future action, then the use of the doer participle ذَاهِب instead of the stateful verb يَذْهَبُ, signifies that the speaker is so sure that he will do this action that he can already be described as a “goer”.

Here are some examples of the doer participle being used to indicate the future tense:

  1. وَقَالَ إِنِّى ذَاهِبٌ إِلَىٰ رَبِّى سَيَهْدِينِ
    [سورة الصافات 37:99]
    And [then] he said, “Indeed, I will go to [where I am ordered by] my Lord; He will guide me.

  2. فَهُوَ ذَاهِبٌ وَتَارِكُهُ لِلنَّاسِ [صحيح مسلم :2959a]
    For he is going to go and leave it for the people.

  3. إِنِّي ذَاكِرٌ لَكَ أَمْرًا [صحيح البخاري :1925]
    I am going to mention to you a matter.

  4. سَأَلْتُ النَّبِيَّ صلى الله عليه وسلم أَنْ يَشْفَعَ لِي يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ فَقَالَ أَنَا فَاعِلٌ [جامع الترمذي :2433]
    I asked the Propher ﷺ that he intercede for me on the Day of Resurrection. So he said, “I will do [it].”

  5. وَإِذْ قَالَ رَبُّكَ لِلْمَلَائِكَةِ إِنِّى جَاعِلٌ فِي ٱلْأَرْضِ خَلِيفَةً [سورة البقرة 2:30]
    And [mention, O Muḥammad], when your Lord said to the angels, “Indeed, I will make upon the earth a successive authority.”

  6. أَلاَ وَإِنِّي تَارِكٌ فِيكُمْ ثَقَلَيْنِ [صحيح مسلم :2408d]
    Unquestionably, I am leaving amongst you two weighty [things]
    (In English, “I am leaving s.th.” can indicate the future tense.)

  7. زَعَمَ ابْنُ أُمِّي أَنَّهُ قَاتِلٌ رَجُلاً قَدْ أَجَرْتُهُ [صحيح البخاري :6158]
    “My maternal brother claims that he will kill a man whom I have given protection to.”

  8. وَإِنِّي مُرْسِلَةٌ إِلَيْهِم بِهَدِيَّةٍ فَنَاظِرَةٌ بِمَ يَرْجِعُ ٱلْمُرْسَلُونَ [سورة النمل 27:35]
    “But indeed, I will send to them a gift and see with what [reply] the messengers will return.”

  9. إِنِّي سَائِلٌ هَذَا عَنْ هَذَا الرَّجُلِ الَّذِي يَزْعُمُ أَنَّهُ نَبِيٌّ [صحيح البخاري :4553]
    I am going to ask this [person] about this man who claims that he is a prophet.

  10. وَيْحَكَ يَا فُلَانُ تَرَى هَذَا كَائِنًا [https://hadithunlocked.com/ahmad:15841]
    A mercy on you O so-and-so! Do you think this will be?

The use of the doer participle instead of the stateful verb to indicate a future event can also, depending on context, indicate that the event is imminent, i.e. that it is in the very near future. In such cases it can be translated using “be going to”. For example:

  1. فَقَالَ عُتْبَةُ لَا شَيْءَ أَنَا خَارِجٌ [maghazi 1/38 cited by Marmorstein, M., Tense and text in Classical Arabic 171]
    So Ɛutbah said, “Nothing. I am going out.”

By the way, it is not just the doer participle which is used to indicate an imminent future event. The stateful verb can also be used with this meaning. For example,

  1. قَالَتْ أَيْنَ قَالَ أُصَلِّي قَالَتِ اجْلِسْ قَالَ إِنِّي أُصَلِّي [صحيح مسلم :560a]
    She said, “Where [are you going]?” He said, “I am going to pray.” She said, “Sit!” He said, “Indeed I am going to pray.”

The doer participle of verbs of posture and verbs of motion don’t have any special behavior when used with a future tense meaning. Examples:

  1. إِنِّي إِنْ شَاءَ اللَّهُ لَقَائِمٌ الْعَشِيَّةَ فِي النَّاسِ، فَمُحَذِّرُهُمْ هَؤُلاَءِ الَّذِينَ يُرِيدُونَ أَنْ يَغْصِبُوهُمْ أُمُورَهُمْ [صحيح البخاري :6830]
    I, if Allāh wills, will stand tonight amongst the peope and warn them [about] these [people] who want to forcibly seize their affairs.

29.4 The doer participle without ٱلْ used to indicate a hypothetical event

The doer participle, is used very commonly in conditional hypothetical clauses with كَانَ. It seems to derive its usage, in most hypothetical clauses, from its future tense meaning. Here are some examples:

  1. وَلَوْ كُنْتُ مُتَّخِذًا مِنَ النَّاسِ خَلِيلاً لاَتَّخَذْتُ أَبَا بَكْرٍ خَلِيلاً [صحيح البخاري :467]
    If I were to take [anyone] from the people as a friend, I would have taken Abū Bakr as a friend.

  2. لَوْ كُنْتُ مُصَلِّيًا قَبْلَهَا أَوْ بَعْدَهَا لَأَتْمَمْتُهَا [https://hadithunlocked.com/ahmad:5185]
    If I were to pray before it or after it, I would have completed them.

  3. وَلَوْ كَانَ مُحَمَّدٌ صلى الله عليه وسلم كَاتِمًا شَيْئًا مِمَّا أُنْزِلَ عَلَيْهِ لَكَتَمَ هَذِهِ الآيَةَ [صحيح مسلم :177b]
    And if Muḥammad were to have concealed anything from what was revealed to him, he would have concealed this āyah.

  4. لَوْ كُنْتُ قَاتِلًا رَسُولًا لَضَرَبْتُ أَعْنَاقَكُمَا [https://hadithunlocked.com/ahmad:3708]
    If I were (to kill/a killer of) a messenger I would have struck your necks.

  5. لَوْ كُنْتُ آمِرًا أَحَدًا أَنْ يَسْجُدَ لأَحَدٍ لأَمَرْتُ الْمَرْأَةَ أَنْ تَسْجُدَ لِزَوْجِهَا [جامع الترمذي :1159]
    If I were to order anyone that he prostrate to anyone, I would have ordered the woman to prostrate to her spouse

  6. لَوْ كَانَ لَكَ مَا عَلَى الْأَرْضِ مِنْ شَيْءٍ أَكُنْتَ مُفْتَدِيًا بِهِ [https://hadithunlocked.com/ahmad:12289]
    If you had everything that is on the earth would you ransom yourself with it?

  7. إِنْ كُنْتَ لاَ بُدَّ فَاعِلاً فَوَاحِدَةً [صحيح مسلم :546]
    If you must do it then (only) once.

The stateful verb is also used in such clauses, but it tends to indicate a hypothetical current situation. For example:

  1. لَوْ كُنْتُ أَقْرَبُهَا أَوْ أَدْخُلُ عَلَيْهَا لأَتَيْتُهَا حَتَّى تُشَافِهَنِي بِهِ [صحيح مسلم :746a]
    If (the current situtation were such that) I were nearing her or entering upon her, I would have come to her so that she would [tell] it to me face-to-face.

  2. قُل لَّوْ كَانَ فِي ٱلْأَرْضِ مَلَائِكَةٌ يَمْشُونَ مُطْمَئِنِّينَ لَنَزَّلْنَا عَلَيْهِم مِّنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَلَكًا رَّسُولًا [سورة الإسراء 17:95]
    Say, “If there were upon the earth angels walking securely, We would have sent down to them from the heaven an angel [as a] messenger.”

  3. إِنَّ عَبْدَ اللَّهِ رَجُلٌ صَالِحٌ لَوْ كَانَ يُكْثِرُ الصَّلاَةَ مِنَ اللَّيْلِ [سنن ابن ماجه :3919]
    Indeed Ɛabd Allāh is a righteous man, if only he would pray much in the night.

The doer participle can also be used in non-conditional hypothetical sentences (see section (kaana)). For example:

  1. يَا سَلَمَةُ أَتُرَاكَ كُنْتَ فَاعِلاً [صحيح مسلم :1807a]
    O Salamah, do you think you could do [it]?

  2. أَوَكُنْتَ فَاعِلًا ذَاكَ [مسند أحمد :61]
    Would you have done that?

The stateful verb is also used in such sentences. For example:

  1. أَنْتَ كُنْتَ تَفْعَلُ ذَلِكَ [صحيح مسلم :1788]
    You would/may have done that

29.5 Interchangeability of the stateful verb and the doer participle without ٱلْ

We have described above, the circumstances when the doer participle instead of the stateful verb,. We would like to state that these are more like trends rather than hard and fast rules. We sometimes find a stateful verb where an doer participle could have worked, and vice versa.

For the indication of a static state, consider, for instance, example (24) (وَهُوَ مُضْطَجِعٌ عَلَى فِرَاشِهِ لاَبِسٌ مِرْطَ عَائِشَةَ) above and compare it with with example (85) below:

  1. فِإِنِّي رَأَيْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صلى الله عليه وسلم يَلْبَسُ النِّعَالَ الَّتِي لَيْسَ فِيهَا شَعْرٌ [سنن أبي داود :1772]
    “I have seen the Messenger of Allah ﷺ wearing the sandals which don’t have hair on them (i.e. tanned).”

Consider also the following two narrations:

  1. … وَلاَ آخَرُ قَدِ اشْتَرَى غَنَمًا أَوْ خَلِفَاتٍ وَهُوَ مُنْتَظِرٌ وِلاَدَهَا [صحيح مسلم :1747]
    “… nor another who has bought sheep or pregnant she-camels and he is waiting for their giving birth”

  2. … وَلاَ أَحَدٌ اشْتَرَى غَنَمًا أَوْ خَلِفَاتٍ وَهْوَ يَنْتَظِرُ وِلاَدَهَا [صحيح البخاري :3124]
    “… nor one who has bought sheep or pregnant she-camels and he is waiting for their giving birth”

Similarly, for the indication of the future tense, contrast example (@ism_fail_going_to_my_lord) with example (88) below:

  1. فَقَالَ يَا أُنَيْسُ أَذَهَبْتَ حَيْثُ أَمَرْتُكَ قَالَ قُلْتُ نَعَمْ أَنَا أَذْهَبُ يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ [صحيح مسلم :2310a]
    He said “O Unays, did you go where I commanded you [to go]?” I said, “Yes I am going, O Messenger of Allah”

Consider also the following two narrations:

  1. أَرَأَيْتَكُمْ لَوْ أَخْبَرْتُكُمْ أَنَّ خَيْلاً تَخْرُجُ بِسَفْحِ هَذَا الْجَبَلِ أَكُنْتُمْ مُصَدِّقِيَّ [صحيح مسلم :208a]
    “Do you think if I were to inform you that there were horsemen emerging out of the foot of this mountain, would you believe me?”

  2. أَرَأَيْتُمْ إِنْ حَدَّثْتُكُمْ أَنَّ الْعَدُوَّ مُصَبِّحُكُمْ أَوْ مُمَسِّيكُمْ، أَكُنْتُمْ تُصَدِّقُونِي [صحيح البخاري :4972]
    “Do you think if I told you that an enemy is going to attack you in the morning or in the evening, will you believe me?”

29.6 The doer participle without ٱلْ used with a perfect significance

We have seen, in the sections above, that the doer participle without ٱلْ has a strong correlation with the u-state stateful verb, giving an imperfect meaning. But, the doer participle without ٱلْ can also carry the significance of the perfect verb. Such that it can indicate that the doer has completed the action of the verb. As when the doer participle was used in an imperfect sense, here too it can indicate being in a static state. Examples:

  1. لَا آتِيكَ مُجْرِمًا [Fischer 112]
    I shall not come to you as one who has committed a crime.
    (مُجْرِمًا is here in place of the perfect verb وَقَدْ أَجْرَمْتُ.)

  2. جِيءَ بِالنُّعَيْمَانِ أَوِ ابْنِ النُّعَيْمَانِ شَارِبًا [صحيح البخاري :2316]
    al-Nuɛaymān or al-Nuɛaymān’s son was brought (in a state of) of having drunk (wine).

  3. قَالَ جَابِرٌ فَبَيْنَا أَنَا نَازِلٌ تَحْتَ شَجَرَةٍ إِذَا رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صلى الله عليه وسلم [موطأ مالك :48]
    “While I was (resting) dismounted under a tree, the Messenger of Allah ﷺ [came].”

  4. لَوْ كُنْتُ مُتَوَضِّئًا أَكَلْتُهُ [https://hadithunlocked.com/ahmad:15147, graded weak by al-ʾArnaʾūṭ]
    If I were in a state of having done wuḍūʾ, I would have eaten it.

When used with a perfect significance, then, according to most authorities, the doer participle cannot usually have a direct doee. (See section (ref).)

29.7 The doer participle defined by ٱل

When the doer participle is defined by ٱل and has verbal force, then the ٱل serves an identifying purpose, corresponding to the connected nouns (ٱلَّذِي, ٱلَّتِي, etc.). And the doer participle itself has a strong correspondence with the verb. So for example, the doer participle القَاتِل corresponds to the structure الَّذِي قَتَلَ/يَقْتُلُ. The meaning of the verb is included in the meaning of the doer participle. So much so, that an actual verb may be conjuncted to the meaning of the verb that is included in the doer participle2: ٱل. For example:

  1. إِنَّ ٱلْمُصَّدِّقِينَ وَٱلْمُصَّدِّقَاتِ وَأَقْرَضُوا ٱللَّهَ قَرْضًا حَسَنًا يُضَاعَفُ لَهُمْ وَلَهُمْ أَجْرٌ كَرِيمٌ [سورة الحديد 57:18]
    “Indeed, the men who practice charity and the women who practice charity and [they who] have loaned Allāh a goodly loan - it will be multiplied for them, and they will have a noble reward.”

29.8 Verbs for which the doer participle is rarely used with verbal force

The doer participle, when signifying verbal force, is only rarely used for some verbs. These include verbs like رَأَى to see, رَجَا to hope, أَرَادَ to want, and others. For such verbs, the stateful verb is typically used when verbal force is needed. Nevertheless, the doer participle for such verbs has been found to be used on occasion. Examples:

  1. أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ص لَمَّا انْصَرَفَ مِنَ الطَّائِفِ مُرِيدًا مَكَّةَ مَرَّ بِهِ بَعْضُ أَهْلِ مَكَّةَ [https://shamela.ws/book/9783/972]
    that the Messenger of Allah, when he departed from al-Ṭāʾif seeking Makkah, one of the people of Makkah passed by him

  2. أُرَانِي اللَّيْلَةَ عِنْدَ الْكَعْبَةِ فَرَأَيْتُ رَجُلاً آدَمَ كَأَحْسَنِ مَا أَنْتَ رَاءٍ مِنْ أُدْمِ الرِّجَالِ [صحيح البخاري :6999]
    I saw myself (in a dream) near the Kaɛbah last night, and I saw a man with whitish red complexion, the best you may see amongst men of that complexion

29.9 Applicability of the doer participle to the doer

In this section, we will discuss at what point the doer of a verb may be ascribed by the doer participle. For example, if a person reads or recites only one word, can he be described as a قَارِئ? Or does the person need to read/recite a large amount of text to qualify being called a قَارِئ?

In theory, the doer participle has a temporary applicability that is tied to the occurence of a verb. For example, theoretically, any person who has performed, is performing, or will perform an action of the verb قَرَأَ, even once, can be called a قَارِئ. This is a situational applicability. And from context it will be understood that the person is being called a قَارِئ within the situation of a certain reading or recitation. For example:

  1. أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صلى الله عليه وسلم صَلَّى الظُّهْرَ فَجَعَلَ رَجُلٌ يَقْرَأُ خَلْفَهُ بِسَبِّحِ اسْمَ رَبِّكَ الأَعْلَى فَلَمَّا انْصَرَفَ قَالَ أَيُّكُمْ قَرَأَ أَوْ أَيُّكُمُ الْقَارِئُ [صحيح مسلم :398b]
    that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ prayed the ḍ͡huhr prayer, and a man began to recite behind him [sūrat al-Aɛlā]. When [the Prophet ﷺ concluded the prayer and] turned around, he said: “Which of you recited?” or “Which of you [was] the reciter?”

  2. سَمِعَ النَّبِيُّ صلى الله عليه وسلم قَارِئًا يَقْرَأُ مِنَ اللَّيْلِ فِي الْمَسْجِدِ [صحيح البخاري :5042]
    The Prophet ﷺ heard a reciter reciting the Qurʾān in the mosque at night.

However, in practice, a person may have done the action of the verb many times, or is skilful in it, or does the action as his profession. In these cases he can be described by the doer participle generally. For example:

  1. وَقَرَأْتَ الْقُرْآنَ لِيُقَالَ هُوَ قَارِئٌ [صحيح مسلم :1905a]
    And you recited the Qurʾān in order that it be said: “He is a reciter.”

  2. عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ عُبَيْدٍ وَكَانَ يُدْعَى فِي زَمَنِ النَّبِيِّ صلى الله عليه وسلم الْقَارِئَ [https://hadithunlocked.com/tabarani:5540]
    [It was narrated] from Saɛīd ibn Ɛubayd, and he was called in the time of the Prophet ﷺ the reciter…

For stylistic purposes, the doer participle can also be contrasted with the verb. Such that the doer participle will signify a description the doer, or his state. Whereas the verb will signify only the occurence of an action. Consider for example, سورة الكافرون:

قُلۡ یَـٰۤأَیُّهَا ٱلۡكَـٰفِرُونَ۝ لَاۤ أَعۡبُدُ مَا تَعۡبُدُونَ۝ وَلَاۤ أَنتُمۡ عَـٰبِدُونَ مَاۤ أَعۡبُدُ۝ وَلَاۤ أَنَا۠ عَابِدࣱ مَّا عَبَدتُّمۡ۝ وَلَاۤ أَنتُمۡ عَـٰبِدُونَ مَاۤ أَعۡبُدُ۝ لَكُمۡ دِینُكُمۡ وَلِیَ دِینِ۝

[سورة الكافرون]
Say, “O disbelievers, I do not worship what you worship. Nor are you worshippers of what I worship. Nor will I be a worshipper of what you worship. Nor will you be worshippers of what I worship. For you is your religion, and for me is my religion.”

Ibn al-Qayyim says, in the tafsīr of this sūrah, that the Prophet ﷺ is negated from worshipping the disbelievers’ gods using once the verb أَعْبُدُ and once using the doer participle عَابِد. So the Prophet ﷺ is disassociated from their false gods from every perspective and at every time. For the negated verb لَا أَعْبُدُ negates the occurence of the action of worshipping and its continuation from the Prophet ﷺ. And the negated doer participle وَ لَا أَنَا عَابِدٌ states that the Prophet ﷺ cannot be described as a worshipper of their false gods, or as being in a state of worshipping their false gods.

Whereas the disbelievers are negated from worshipping Allāh both times using the negated doer participle ولا أنْتُمْ عَابِدُون. This is because, even if they had sometimes worshipped Allāh along with their false gods, they do not qualify being described as “worshippers of Allāh”. This epithet is only applicable to one who worships Allāh without associating any partners with him.3

29.10 The doer participle as an adjectival noun

So far, we have focused on the doer participle being used with verbal force. But some doer participles may also be used as adjectival nouns. For example, عَادِل “just”, وَاسِع “spacious”, غَاضِب “angry”, جَائِع “hungry”, etc. And, depending on the situation and root meaning, they can have a temporary or permanent applicability.

Theoretically, a noun on the pattern of the doer participle exists for all verbs. So from the verb, رَبِحَ “to be profitable” we get رَابِح, from the verb, ضَاقَ “to be narrow” we get ضَائِق, etc. In practice, not all doer participles are commonly used as adjectival nouns. So, for example, رَابِح is commonly used as a adjectival noun to mean “profitable”. But ضَائِق is not commonly used as a adjectival noun to mean “narrow”. The participle-like adjective ضَيِّق “narrow” is used instead. Having said that, the doer participle, even when largely unused as a adjectival noun may, on occasion, be used as a adjectival noun, for stylistic purposes, to indicate the temporariness of a state. The participle-like adjective, on the other hand, typically indicates a more permanent state. Consider for example:

  1. فَلَعَلَّكَ تَارِكُۢ بَعۡضَ مَا یُوحَىٰۤ إِلَیۡكَ وَضَاۤىِٕقُۢ بِهِۦ صَدۡرُكَ أَن یَقُولُوا۟ لَوۡلَاۤ أُنزِلَ عَلَیۡهِ كَنزٌ أَوۡ جَاۤءَ مَعَهُۥ مَلَكٌۚ إِنَّمَاۤ أَنتَ نَذِیرࣱۚ وَٱللَّهُ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَیۡءࣲ وَكِیلٌ

    [سورة هود 11:12]
    Then would you possibly leave [out] some of what is revealed to you, or is your breast constrained by it because they say, “Why has there not been sent down to him a treasure or come with him an angel?” But you are only a warner. And Allāh is Disposer of all things.

In the above āyah ضَائِق has been used instead of the more common ضَيِّق to indicate the temporariness of its meaning.4
